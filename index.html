Phase 3 · why it matters

Why the Philippine corridor needs a direct provider

Gaian's current Philippine coverage runs through a resold, non-direct provider — an intermediary layer meaning higher fees, slower settlement, and no direct control over the payout rail.

This grant integrates a direct, institution-grade provider

  • Lower fees — no intermediary markup
  • Faster settlement — direct rail, not resold
  • Regulatory-grade reliability + direct reconciliation
  • Control to scale real merchant payouts at volume

Why it's a distinct deliverable

A first-party settlement relationship is a fresh integration — new API, settlement, compliance, treasury — and it's what makes the corridor safe to build merchant payments on.

Committed proof: 50 transactions paying Philippine merchants, settled from Stellar USDC.
